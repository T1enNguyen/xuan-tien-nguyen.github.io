# Introduction to My Digital Garden

Learning is not just about consuming information; it's about connecting ideas. In this post, I'll share how I've set up my **Obsidian** vault to feed into this Jekyll website.

## Why Obsidian?
Obsidian allows for:
- **Bi-directional linking**: Connecting concepts easily.
- **Local-first storage**: I own my data.
- **Markdown support**: Perfect for Jekyll!

## Structuring Notes
I follow the [Zettelkasten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zettelkasten) method loosely:
1. **Fleeting Notes**: Random thoughts.
2. **Literature Notes**: Summaries of books or articles.
3. **Permanent Notes**: Well-reasoned ideas.

## Syncing with GitHub Pages
Since my website is hosted on GitHub Pages, I simply sync my Obsidian vault (or a specific folder within it) to this repository. Every time I push a change, Jekyll rebuilds the site!

> "The more you link, the more you think."
